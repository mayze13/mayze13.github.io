Hello, I'm James, born in December 1997.

My childhood was a lively journey back and forth between France and Scotland, enriched by a bilingual upbringing.
You should see our family conversations at the dinner table: a symphony of Franglish, peppered with a Scottish accent. It's quite unique

I was always a bit nerdy (in a good way), enjoyed geeking out on atlases as a kid, and got interested in geography and mathematics from a young age.
Since I was quite good at school, I took up geography at university, where I studied its human aspects such as cities, demographics, and politics.
The physical aspects like rock formations and plate tectonics didn't capture my interest as much.

I had the opportunity to study abroad in Canada, where I delved way deeper into the "human aspect" through electives.
My curiosity was increasingly shifting towards the biological underpinnings of human behaviour, rather than the macro, societal underpinnings.
After graduating with my Bachelor's degree, I enacted my rite of passage into manhood by studying wild monkeys in the tropical rainforest of Malaysia.

Although ethology and primate behaviour still fascinate me today, my interest was always more towards human behaviour. Therefore, I set sail to Amsterdam where I enrolled in the Masters of Brain and Cognitive Science.
I had the opportunity to work with brilliant scientists in Berlin, and took a particular liking to the topic of Environmental Neuroscience.
No, no - this isn't understanding the effects of greenwashing on the brain, or the neural reward system of correctly sorting your waste. Rather, it's about how our psychology and cognition are affected by the world around us: our cities, our landscapes, our offices, our homes.
It's an elegant combination of geography and neuroscience, in my view.

After obtaining my Master's degree, I embarked on the Data Science Bootcamp at Le Wagon, seeing it as an extra, hands-on skill set, complementing the theoretical knowledge I had acquired.
I am now based in France, where I divide my time between Bordeaux and La Rochelle. I enjoy escaping to Lisbon as often as I can.
I've worked on a couple of Data Science and AI projects already, and I am eager to delve deeper and build a career in this blossoming field.

Jazz pianist, language enthusiast, avid sci-fi reader stuck at 1800 in chess, and aspiring AI engineer/scientist/consultant. Welcome!
